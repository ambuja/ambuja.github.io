As a working, productive Farm, every animal on our Farm provides us with a useful product or service, Our animals are raised humanely, They are allowed to graze and roam on organic pastures and fed on organic grains, hay, and grass.We keep and raise animals for

• meat: beef, pork, rabbit, chicken, turkey, goat, lamb

• eggs: chickens

• wool: sheep

• garden fertilizer:lweed control and ground clearing: goats, sheep, pig

• pest control: cat, dogs

• teaching people about animals!

 

Animals in Residence

Cows
We offer beef for sale approximately every other fall.
Sheep
Three black ewes,  three white ewes. One black ewe and one white ewe are Dorsets. All females are bred in the fall. Lambs are born in late winter/early spring, and sold for meat in late spring.
 
Goats
lippy (brown Nubian),  Mally (Saanen doe), Eddy the billy goat. Does (females) are bred in the fall. Kids are born in the late winter/early spring and sold for meat in late spring. Female goats who have birthed can be milked until they are bred again. We do not sell goats' milk.
 
Pigs
4: We have four feeder pigs and one sow.
 
Chickens
100 Layers & Broilers. Eggs are available year-round. They are $6/dozen. The best times to get them are at chore time 9 AM and 4 PM. Broilers (meat birds) are available in the autumn on a limited basis. They are sold frozen. Limit 5 per family.
 
Turkeys
150 Holland Turkeys. Thanksgiving turkeys may be pre-ordered May through November. They are sold frozen. NCOF members have priority August 1 throughl October 15. Birds always sell out. Please see our turkey page for additional information.
 
Rabbits
20 New Zealand White, Rex, and other mixed breeds. Bunnies for pets are sold in spring, summer, and fall. You must visit the Farm Monday through Friday (not on weekends) from 9 AM to 4:30 PM to purchase a rabbit and sign adoption papers. Sorry, but we cannot take reservations for specific rabbits. Call to check availability. Note: We do not accept donations of rabbits and we do not take adopted rabbits back.
